About me

I specialize in the study of the development, progression, and therapy of colorectal cancer (CRC), placing my main focus on this area. CRC arises due to the accumulation of multiple genetic and epigenetic alterations. Epigenetic regulation is now recognized as a crucial hallmark of CRC. Through the use of high-resolution technologies and translational evidence from bedside-to-bench, I investigate the functionality of epigenetic-fueled proteins to uncover valuable insights into CRC progression. As such, my research addresses two interconnected questions: 1. How do epigenetic changes contribute to the development of CRC and impact the cancer environment? 2. What lessons can we derive from preclinical research to discover novel drugs and therapies? By delving into these inquiries, I strive to advance our understanding of CRC and pave the way for innovative approaches in drug development and treatment strategies.
